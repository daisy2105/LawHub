CONSTITUTIONAL LAW – KEY POINTS

• The Constitution is the supreme law of India.
• It defines the framework of government, powers, and fundamental rights of citizens.
• Divided into parts: Fundamental Rights, Directive Principles, Fundamental Duties, etc.
• Fundamental Rights (Part III): Protect individual liberty (Articles 12–35).
• Directive Principles (Part IV): Guide the State toward social and economic justice.
• Fundamental Duties (Article 51A): Responsibilities of citizens.
• Separation of powers: Legislature, Executive, Judiciary – Articles 50, 121, 122, etc.
• Amendment power – Article 368.
• Supreme Court is guardian of the Constitution – Article 32.
