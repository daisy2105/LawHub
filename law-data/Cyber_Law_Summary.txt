CYBER LAW – KEY POINTS

• Cyber law deals with offences and regulations related to cyberspace, internet, and digital communication.
• Covers hacking, identity theft, cyberstalking, online fraud, data protection, and privacy.
• IT Act, 2000 – main legislation governing electronic transactions and cybercrimes.
• Electronic signatures and digital evidence recognized under the Act.
• Constitutional provisions relevant to cyber law:
  – Article 19(1)(a): Freedom of speech and expression online.
  – Article 21: Right to privacy and protection of personal data.
  – Article 32: Constitutional remedies for violation of rights.
  – Article 51A(h): Duty to develop scientific temper and respect for digital ethics.
• Ensures security and accountability in online communication and commerce.
• Promotes safe digital environment and protection of digital identity.
