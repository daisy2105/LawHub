COMMERCIAL LAW – KEY POINTS

• Commercial law regulates business and trade-related activities.
• Includes laws on contracts, companies, partnerships, negotiable instruments, and sales of goods.
• Aims to ensure fair trade, competition, and protection of stakeholders.
• Key Acts: Companies Act, 2013; Contract Act, 1872; Sale of Goods Act, 1930; Partnership Act, 1932; Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881.
• Constitutional link: Article 19(1)(g) – Freedom to practice any trade or business; Article 301 – Freedom of trade and commerce.
• Helps build investor confidence and supports economic growth.
• Encourages corporate responsibility and accountability.
