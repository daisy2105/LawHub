ENVIRONMENTAL LAW – KEY POINTS

• Focuses on protection and improvement of the natural environment.
• Aims to control pollution, conserve biodiversity, and promote sustainable development.
• Key Acts: Environment (Protection) Act, 1986; Water Act, 1974; Air Act, 1981; Wildlife Protection Act, 1972.
• Constitutional provisions:
  – Article 48A: Duty of State to protect environment and wildlife.
  – Article 51A(g): Duty of citizens to protect environment.
  – Article 21: Right to a healthy environment (judicial interpretation).
• Promotes balance between development and environmental protection.
