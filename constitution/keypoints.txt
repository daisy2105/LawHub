Constitution Key Points - LawHub

=== Constitutional Law ===
- Fundamental Rights (Articles 12-35): Right to equality, freedom, life & personal liberty, religious freedom, and remedies.
- Directive Principles: Non-justiciable principles guiding governance and social welfare.
- Separation of Powers: Legislature, Executive, Judiciary roles and checks & balances.
- Basic Structure Doctrine: Certain features of the Constitution cannot be amended.

=== Criminal Law ===
- Principles: Mens rea (guilty mind), actus reus (guilty act), procedural safeguards.
- Criminal Procedure: Arrest, investigation, bail, charge framing, trial, sentencing, appeals.
- Rights of accused: Presumption of innocence, right to counsel, fair trial.

=== Civil Law ===
- Contracts: Offer, acceptance, consideration, capacity, breach, remedies.
- Torts & Remedies: Negligence, nuisance, defamation, remedies—damages, injunctions.
- Civil Procedure: Pleadings, jurisdiction, limitation, evidence rules.

=== Commercial Law ===
- Business Entities: Formation, duties, liability of partnerships and companies.
- Contractual obligations: Sale, agency, negotiable instruments, commercial disputes.
- Regulation: Consumer protection and corporate compliance.

=== Family Law ===
- Marriage & Divorce: Grounds, procedures, maintenance, alimony.
- Child Welfare: Custody, adoption, guardianship principles.
- Personal laws: Variations by religion and applicable statutes.

=== Environmental Law ===
- Polluter pays principle and precautionary principle.
- Regulatory framework: Environmental impact assessments, pollution control boards.
- Public interest litigation and judicial oversight in environmental protection.

=== Cyber Law ===
- Digital offences: Hacking, data breaches, identity theft.
- Data protection principles: Privacy, consent, lawful processing.
- Electronic evidence: Admissibility and chain-of-custody concerns.

=== Consumer Protection ===
- Consumer rights: Safety, information, choice, redressal mechanisms.
- Remedies: Complaints to consumer forums and compensation claims.
- Unfair trade practices and defective goods handling.

=== Labour Law ===
- Worker rights: Wages, safe working conditions, social security.
- Industrial relations: Trade unions, collective bargaining, dispute resolution.
- Employment contracts and termination safeguards.

=== Administrative Law ===
- Rule-making and delegated legislation: Limits and guidance.
- Judicial review: Grounds - illegality, irrationality, unfairness.
- Principles of natural justice: Fair hearing, absence of bias.
